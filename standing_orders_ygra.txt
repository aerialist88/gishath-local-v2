Win cons: grind opponents out with repeated forced sacrifice (Ygra's swarm-eat trigger, Dictate of Erebos, Grave Pact, Sheoldred's Edict, Bastion of Remembrance) turning into direct damage/drain via Blood Artist, Zulaport Cutthroat, Nadier's Nightblade, Disciple of the Vault, Ravenous Squirrel. Secondary line: Marionette Master + sac outlets for a lethal one-turn ping burst. Ygra herself grows into a huge eldrazi-sized threat that can just attack for lethal once fed enough creatures/counters.
Ygra needs a wide, expendable board to eat — do not attack with her or feed her into a bad combat until you have counterspell/removal backup or a clear kill; she is a value engine first, finisher second.
Sac outlets (Viscera Seer, Carrion Feeder, Ashnod's Altar, Pitiless Plunderer, High Market, Phyrexian Tower, Miren the Moaning Well) are only worth activating when there is a real payoff on the stack: a creature about to die anyway, an aristocrats trigger (Blood Artist/Zulaport/Disciple) that matters, feeding Ygra for a real size/kill purpose, or Ravenous Squirrel/Carrion Feeder counters toward lethal. Do not sac creatures for no reason just because mana is open.
Hold Dictate of Erebos and Grave Pact until your board has more expendable/token creatures than opponents' boards, or hold as a blowout for when you expect to lose creatures to a wipe anyway — casting them onto a board where you have fewer creatures than opponents just hurts you.
Bastion of Remembrance and similar "on death, drain" enchantments: play proactively early, they're passive value, no need to hold.
Toxic Deluge and Blasphemous Edict are your board wipes — hold them for when you are behind on board or facing a lethal alpha strike, not when your own board is bigger (a wipe on your best turn is a wasted card). Pay minimum life with Toxic Deluge, just enough to clear the real threats.
Assassin's Trophy, Beast Within, Putrefy, Abrupt Decay, Bake into a Pie, Fell the Profane, Deadly Rollick: hold as reactive removal for the single biggest threat (opposing commander, combo piece, a creature about to kill you), not for random small blockers. Deadly Rollick specifically should be held at instant speed for a creature spell resolving, not cast proactively.
Heroic Intervention and Snakeskin Veil: hold up mana turn 4+ once you have a real board, cast only in response to a board wipe or removal aimed at your key piece (Ygra, a combo enabler) — never cast proactively with nothing to protect.
Demonic Tutor and Vampiric Tutor: hold until you know exactly what piece wins or stabilizes the game (a wipe, a sac outlet, Grave Pact-style effect, or lethal). Don't tutor early for generic value pieces.
Reanimate and Victimize: hold for when Ygra or another big payoff creature (Feasting Troll King, Grim Haruspex, Marionette Master) is in the graveyard, ideally right after it dies rather than reanimating a random small creature.
Morbid Opportunist, Grim Haruspex, Midnight Reaper: card draw engines that trigger off death — deploy on curve, they want the sac/death subtheme already running, not defensive holds.
Ophiomancer, Pitiless Plunderer, Warren Soultrader, Tireless Provisioner: token/treasure generators, play on curve, feed the sac outlets and Ygra naturally, no need to hold.
Ramp (Llanowar Elves, Elvish Mystic, Birds of Paradise, Delighted Halfling, Elves of Deep Shadow, Sakura-Tribe Elder, Kodama's Reach, Gilded Goose): play turns 1-3 on sight, these are also fine sac fodder later once their ramp job is done.
Sakura-Tribe Elder: hold the sac activation until blocking or until you need the land immediately, don't sac it proactively before combat unless it's chump-blocking a real threat.
Ashnod's Altar and Pitiless Plunderer combo with sac triggers for mana/treasure — activate Ashnod's Altar for mana only when you have a spell/ability worth casting with it that turn, not just to empty the board.
Deadly Dispute and Night's Whisper: card draw, cast whenever mana allows, Deadly Dispute is best sac'ing a creature that already did its job (ramp dork, Sakura-Tribe Elder post-block) not your best creature.
Rise of the Witch-king and Braids, Arisen Nightmare: sacrifice/attrition engines, deploy proactively once you have board presence to abuse them; Braids' upkeep trigger is best used when opponents have less to lose than you do.
Mazirek, Kraul Death Priest and Marionette Master want counters from death triggers — sequence sac effects after these resolve, not before, to maximize counters/damage.
Bane of Progress: hold until it clearly 1-for-many's opposing artifacts/enchantments (and check your own board isn't bigger loser) — don't fire it into a symmetrical or bad trade.
Blasphemous Edict scales down with fewer creatures on the strongest board — time it for when an opponent has overextended, not when boards are even.
Natural posture: this deck is a grindy attrition/aristocrats deck, not a fast racer. Develop board and death-trigger engines turns 1-5, avoid overextending into wipes, force trades via sac effects, and close the game turns 7+ once Ygra is huge or a Grave Pact/Dictate lock plus sac outlet creates a killing edict loop.
In 4-player pods, let other players trade removal/wipes first; you profit from board wipes if you can rebuild faster via recursion (Eternal Witness, Reanimate) — don't be the one holding the most creatures when a wipe is likely.
Prioritize protecting/growing Ygra and your sac-payoff creatures (Blood Artist, Zulaport Cutthroat, Disciple of the Vault) over chip damage; losing a payoff piece early kills the whole plan.
If an opponent is about to wipe the board, proactively sac your own creatures into your outlets first to get death-trigger value before the wipe takes them for free.
RACE TRIGGER: two or more aristocrats payoffs (Blood Artist/Zulaport Cutthroat/Nadier's Nightblade/Disciple of the Vault/Grave Pact/Dictate of Erebos) live simultaneously with 3+ expendable creatures/tokens on board — sacrifice and attack every turn until an opponent is dead.
