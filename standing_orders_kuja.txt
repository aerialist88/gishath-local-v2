Win cons: big Exsanguinate/Boltwave/City on Fire X-spell after a mana burst, or grinding chip damage via Guttersnipe/Ashling/Storm-Kiln Artist/Electro triggers off cheap spells. Kuja backside (Trance Kuja) wants you casting many spells in a turn to flip and close games.
Core mana-burst line: Dark Ritual, Seething Song, Mana Geyser, Battle Hymn, Jeska's Will feed one big turn. Hold these until you also hold (or will draw into) a payoff — do not crack them just to play a 2-drop early.
Do not fire Dark Ritual/Seething Song/Mana Geyser on curve turns 1-3 unless immediately casting Kuja or a ritual chain into real damage. Wasting them empty-handed loses the game.
Sol Ring, Arcane Signet, Talisman of Indulgence, Ruby Medallion, Command Tower/War Room/Rakdos Carnarium: play on sight, always good, no need to hold.
Hold Chaos Warp, Terminate, Bedevil, Saw in Half, Cornered by Black Mages, Tragic Slip as removal for the biggest threat on board (commander, combo piece, flier alpha-striking you) — do not spend on small creatures.
Hold Not Dead After All and Feign Death for when Kuja or another key creature is about to die or already died — reanimate at end of opponent's turn if possible, not proactively.
Deflecting Swat and Transpose are reactive tricks: hold up mana, use only in response to a targeted removal/counter spell aimed at your stuff, or to redirect a wipe/bomb spell. Never cast Deflecting Swat with no valid target on the stack.
Lightning Greaves and Command Beacon protect Kuja specifically — equip Greaves to Kuja the turn you play it or the turn before you tap out for a big spell, not on random creatures.
Chandra's Ignition and City on Fire are one-shot damage plays: cast only when you can point them at an opponent (or board) for lethal or near-lethal, not as removal on a single blocker.
Wild Magic Surge and Untimely Malfunction are swingy/random — only cast when behind or when the board favors chaos (multiple opponents developed, you are not overextended into a wipe).
Exsanguinate and Boltwave: hold as finishers, cast for max X only, ideally after a ritual turn; do not cast for X=1-2 just to "get value."
Guttersnipe, Ashling Flame Dancer, Storm-Kiln Artist, Electro, Coruscation Mage: deploy early, they are engines not targets to protect at all costs — fine to lose them after they've triggered a few times.
Faithless Looting, Light Up the Stage, Sign in Blood, Demand Answers: card draw/filtering, cast whenever mana is free, no need to hold.
Fiery Emancipation: play only when you have a follow-up burn spell ready same turn or next turn to abuse the damage tripling; otherwise it's a do-nothing that invites removal.
Neheb, the Eternal and Birgi/Harnfel: mana/card engines, play on curve, they enable the ritual-into-X-spell line.
Activated abilities (Black Mage's Rod, Cursed Mirror, Boggart Trawler, Mysidian Elder, Harmonic Prodigy): only activate when there is a concrete payoff this turn (lethal push, saving a creature, enabling a combat trick) — do not activate on autopilot every turn if mana is otherwise needed for spells in hand.
Circle of Power: hold mana up specifically to protect against a board wipe or a lethal spell aimed at you; don't tap out if you have it and expect a wipe.
The Darkness Crystal: ramp/fixing, play early, low risk.
Natural posture: this deck is a control-into-burst-kill deck, not an early racer. Durdle behind removal and card draw turns 1-4, avoid overextending into wipes, then look for the turn you can chain rituals into a lethal X-spell or alpha strike, usually turns 5-7.
In 4-player pods, avoid being the first to tap out for a huge turn unless it's actually lethal or near-lethal to at least one opponent — partial commitments just paint a target on you.
Prioritize killing the most dangerous opposing threat/commander over dealing incidental damage; hold single-target removal for that rather than using it reactively on small stuff.
If Kuja dies, prioritize the reanimation spells (Not Dead After All/Feign Death) over recasting other creatures that turn.
