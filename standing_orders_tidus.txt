Win conditions: +1/+1 counter overrun via Tekuthal/Hardened Scales/Forgotten Ancient stacking counters, then Herald of Secret Streams or Kodama of the West Tree makes creatures unblockable for lethal. Secondary: Fathom Mage/Gyre Sage card-draw-into-mana snowball to cast Sin, Unending Cataclysm or Altered Ego as a huge threat. Tidus commander damage is a backup win, not the plan.
Default posture: develop and go wide/tall with counters turns 1-6, stay reactive, avoid tapping out into open blue/white mana from opponents. Do not attack recklessly into bigger boards before counters or unblockable is online.
Hold Mana Leak, Narset's Reversal, Protection Magic for opponents' board wipes, extra-turn spells, or their own combo pieces — never counter a small utility spell on turn 2-3.
Hold Path to Exile / An Offer You Can't Refuse for the single biggest threat on board, not the first creature played; An Offer especially punishes someone about to combo off or cast a bomb.
Farewell and Destroy Evil are one-sided-wipe tools: only cast when this deck's own board is behind or empty, never when we're ahead on counters/creatures. Farewell can exile our own graveyard/artifacts too, so check mode choice against what we need to keep.
Collective Effort and Resourceful Defense are combat tricks/protection — hold up mana on opponents' turns or during combat, don't cast proactively on our own main phase.
Sword of Truth and Justice, Innkeeper's Talent, Bred for the Hunt: equip/attach only onto a creature that will actually attack or survive combat that turn, not defensively.
Walking Ballista: do not ping it down piece by piece for no reason; save counter removal for finishing off a low-toughness threat or as a last-ditch reach for lethal.
Fathom Mage / Gyre Sage: only tap for mana or value when a counter is about to be added anyway (bounce, proliferate, Forgotten Ancient trigger) — don't sac tempo just to draw one card immediately.
Forgotten Ancient's ability to move counters: use it right before combat to make the actual attacker/blocker big, not preemptively.
Kodama of the West Tree landfall bounce: only bounce Nesting Grounds, bounce-lands, or a land needed for a second land drop — don't bounce a tapped-out dual for no upside.
Nesting Grounds: hold it to relocate a counter from a creature about to die, or to reset an opponent's problematic permanent — not just to tap a land.
Sphere Grid and Path of Discovery: passive value engines, let them sit and trigger; no activation timing decisions needed beyond normal land drops/combat.
Yuna's Whistle and Yuna's Decision: save as blowout/tempo swings — Whistle for a surprise flash blocker or end-of-turn setup, Decision to counter a spell or force a bad attack, not on curve.
Sin, Unending Cataclysm: this is a late-game bomb; don't cast it into an open board wipe or removal-heavy table without protection (Protection Magic/Endless Detour) in hand.
Endless Detour: redirect removal aimed at our best counter-creature or Tidus; hold it, don't preemptively bounce our own stuff.
Bala Ged Recovery // Sanctuary: cast the Recovery side to answer a problem permanent or recur a key card; only flip to Sanctuary land side if mana is tight.
In 4-player pods, avoid being the first to tap out for a big spell when two+ opponents still hold up mana — bait removal/counters with a lesser creature first when possible.
If this deck falls behind on board, prioritize ramp (Farseek, Three Visits, Kodama's Reach, Rampant Rejuvenator) and stabilizing blockers over racing.
If this deck is ahead on counters/creatures, press the attack with unblockable enablers rather than holding back — the counters engine outscales grindy stalls.
Arcane Signet, Sol Ring, Command Tower: play on sight, no judgement needed.
Auron, Kimahri, Wakka, Rikku, Lulu "Guardian" creatures: mostly fine to deploy on curve as bodies/counter-synergy, but hold any with ETB removal/tuck effects for the best available target rather than the first creature seen.
RACE TRIGGER: Herald of Secret Streams or Kodama of the West Tree is in play making the team unblockable/must-be-blocked-by-one, or total board power is 15+ with counters actively stacking — then attack with everything every turn until the game ends.
